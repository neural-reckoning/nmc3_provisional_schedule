Alcohol addiction and Co-Morbid Adult Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder their correlation with Cardiac, Autonomic and Cognitive behavioral Response

K Mrudula

Mrudula, Dr. TN Sathyaprabha, Dr. Kaviraja Udupa, Dr. Arun Kandasamy, Dr. Jamuna Rajeshwaran

Abstract

Background and Rationale: Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a childhood
neurodevelopmental disorder which has a relation with development of substance use disorders in
adulthood. Physiological link and cognitive correlations between the two disorders may involves the
common network activities of the central autonomic network and frontal cortex. Hence there is a
need for further research on the correlation between the cardiac, cognitive and behavioral
parameters.
Materials and Methods: This is a pilot study which is a part of PhD Study titled “Evaluation of
Neurobiological correlates in men with co morbid alcohol use disorder and adult ADHD” and here are
preliminary results. 16 Study subjects (males and aged 18-50 years) admitted to deaddiction
department of NIMHANS were divided into 2 groups with 8 subjects in each group: Alcohol use
disorder (AUD) with/without comorbid Adult ADHD (AUD+AADHD) and compared with Healthy
controls from hospital staff and students of NIMHANS. Study groups were subjected to
Echocardiography, Conventional autonomic function tests, Pupillometry, Go No Go task, stop signal
task and paired pulse transcranial magnetic stimulation paradigms.
Results: AUD and AUD+AADHD groups showed moderate to severe cardiac autonomic dysfunction,
decreased values in parasympathetic activity, end systolic volume, decreased reaction time implying
more impulsivity. Increased sample size might provide some hints on the correlation links between
various neurobiological measures like cardiac Autonomic & Neurobehavioral dysfunction between
Alcohol addiction & Adult ADHD compared to healthy controls.
Conclusion: Our study demonstrates the importance of neurobiological measures that exist between
ADHD and substance use disorders and subsequent need of comprehensive study-based diagnosis for
improvement of their quality of life.
Keywords: ADHD, Alcohol, TMS, stop signal task, Go No Go task, Heart rate variability, Autonomic
functions.
Ethics statement: The experimental protocols were approved by the institutional human ethics
