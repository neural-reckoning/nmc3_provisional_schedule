Panel on Neuroscience as Visual Art

Abstract

Neuroscience has a long history of collaboration with the visual arts. Dating back to Santiago Ram√≥n y Cajal, visual arts have impacted the discovery process in neuroscience both through technique development (e.g., staining techniques derived from photography) and through artistic drawings that gave our first detailed insights into the form and function of neurons. Today, visual arts remains a medium that communicates both the details and the beauty of the brain and it's parts. This panel of visual artists using neuroscience as their inspiration will discuss their approach to developing and creating their art, as well as the role visual arts can play in science and science communication.
