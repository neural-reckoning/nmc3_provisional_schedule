Mathematical representation of neuron-glia networks.

Marco Peña-Garcia

Nelson Castro, Instituto de Matemática Pura e Aplicada

Abstract

Classical synapses are one-one. However, astrocytes play an active role in synapses, tripartite synapse. Besides, astrocytes are organized in networks, building a neuron-glia network. We propose three mathematical representations of a neuron-glia network: upsilon-family, upsilon-multiset, and upsilon-multidigraph. We represent synapses as graphs of a relation on a set of general neurons and define an upsilon-family as an indexed family of them. Then, we generate a multiset from upsilon-family to simplify it. Analogously to multidigraphs, we define isomorphism for upsilon-multisets. As a final simplification, we unnest the upsilon-multiset, getting the upsilon-multidigraph. Although upsilon-multidigraph has not multiple connections, we conjecture that it preserves isomorphism. From edges vertex of an upsilon-multidigraph, we generate a multi-topology of proper submultisets. Additionally, we create a network descriptor from a quasimetric defined over the vertex set of an upsilon-multidigraph. Finally, we roughly create a geometric realization of an upsilon-family in a three-dimensional Euclidean space. We concluded that neuron-glial network representations have a rich mathematical structure.
