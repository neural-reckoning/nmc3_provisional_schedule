The role of habitual behaviour in the corticostriatal pathway in a mouse model of Parkinson disease.

Josefa Zaldivar

Zaldivar-Diez, J, (1) Centro Integral de Neurociencias A.C. (CINAC); Hernandez, LF, (1); Obeso, JA (1); Roeper, J (2) Institute of Neurophysiology, Goethe University Frankfurt

Abstract

The cardinal motor features of Parkinson disease (PD) typically, but often ignored, have a focal onset (Vidaihet, M. et al; 1994). However, the prevailing current Braak hypothesis about the beginning of PD fails to explain this important, focal and somatotopic pattern. Increased corticostriatal activity onto the caudal putamen leads to the perfect scenario for increased levels of α-synuclein to become toxic upon axonal dopaminergic endings (Foffani, G and Obeso, JA; 2018). It is noteworthy that corticostriatal stress in humans might be facilitated/mediated by continuous engagement in automatic/habitual motor performance giving rise to damage of dopaminergic axons in the motor striatum (Hernández, LF. et al; 2015; 2019).

The goal of this project is to unveil the pathological role that habitual behaviour could have in the corticostriatal circuit in a mouse model of PD. To achieve this goal C57BL/6N mice will be used to anatomically define the cortico-striatal circuit of the paw. Then, α-synuclein pre-formed fibrils will be stereotactically injected in this area of the cortex to determine its uptake by dopaminergic striatal terminals. Finally, the role of cortical stress performed by enhanced automatic/habitual behaviour will be studied in these α-synuclein injected mice analysing the electrophysiological activity at the striatal level while animals perform a high demanding automatic/habitual task (FR4 lever press task).

We will describe the results obtained in the anatomical characterization of the corticostriatal circuit of the paw region in C57BL/6N mice.
