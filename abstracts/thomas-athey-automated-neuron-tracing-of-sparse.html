Automated Neuron Tracing of Sparse Fluorescently Labeled Neurons

Thomas Athey

Jeremias Sulam, JHU; Joshua T. Vogelsetin, JHU; Daniel Tward, JHU; Ulrich Mueller, JHU; Michael I. Miller, JHU

Abstract

Recent advances in brain clearing and imaging have made it possible to image entire mammalian brains at sub-micron resolution. These images offer the potential to assemble brain-wide atlases of neuron morphology, but manual neuron tracing remains a bottleneck. Here we present an automated tracing pipeline that leverages a hidden Markov model to allow tracers to intervene in the areas of least confidence, and utilizes dynamic programming to scale to large image data.
