A Spiking Neural Network Model of the Motor Cortex

Lysea Munoz Haggie (she/her)

Abstract

How do networks of neurons in the motor cortex produce coordinated movements? Electrophysiological recordings in the motor areas show irregular spontaneous firing at rest and transient oscillations in neurons during movement preparation and onset. Excitatory and inhibitory activity must occur together in a balanced state to prevent chaotic dynamics. I have built a physiologically inspired, large scale spiking neural network model which replicates the spontaneous neural activity in the motor cortex using the Brian2 simulator toolbox and based on the work of Esser, et al., 2005.
