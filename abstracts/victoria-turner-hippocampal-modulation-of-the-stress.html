Hippocampal Modulation of the Stress Response

Victoria Turner

Mazen Kheirbek, UCSF

Abstract

An essential adaptive function of the nervous system is to organize appropriate physiological and behavioral responses in the face of stressful situations. However, this process can become dysregulated in psychiatric disease. Characterizing the neural circuits that modify the adaptive stress response is essential to understand how the brain regulates responses to stressful stimuli and how that regulation can go awry. Here we use optogenetics, DREADDs, and in vivo calcium imaging to investigate whether the hippocampus modulates hypothalamic CRH stress responses. Our results suggest how the ventral hippocampus (vHPC) exerts top-down control over the paraventricular nucleus of the hypothalamus (PVN) to regulate responses to a stressful foot shock stimulus.
