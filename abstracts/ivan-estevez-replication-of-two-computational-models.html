Replication of two computational models of locomotion of Caenorhabditis elegans in Brian 2

Ivan Estevez

Francisco J. Valverde-Albacete, Carlos III University of Madrid; Carmen Peláez-Moreno, Carlos III University of Madrid

Abstract

The nematode Caenorhabditis elegans has a simple nervous structure yet displays complex behaviours mediated by a sensation-action loop. We report on replicating two experiments based on two different models of the locomotion neural circuits of C. elegans [1, 2]. Both articles present point-neuron models that consider the graded nature of the electrical activity in C. elegans, as opposed to spiking-neuron models—yet both are different. In the first case a non-dimensional model is used for a small subnetwork in which the values of free parameters are found by applying constraints with a genetic algorithm. The second model is based on physiological parameters for cells and synapses, therefore no optimization is needed and almost the complete connectome of C. elegans is simulated—279 out of 302 neurons. The replications are performed in the simulating platform Brian 2, which is available as a Python module and is different from the original methods used. We were able to verify the main hypothesis proposed by each article. In the first case, the production of intrinsic network oscillations by central pattern generators (CPGs) along the ventral nerve cord of the worm. In the second, the existence of a bifurcation in the model state-space produced by a constant sensory input that creates oscillatory behaviour. Nevertheless, we were unable to replicate exact quantitative results for the first model, and obtained contradictory results when performing neural ablations on the second network model.
