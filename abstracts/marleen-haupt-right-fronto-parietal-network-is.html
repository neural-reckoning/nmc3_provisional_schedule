Right fronto-parietal network is associated with phasic alertness in healthy aging

Marleen Haupt

Adriana L. Ruiz-Rizzo; Christian Sorg; Kathrin Finke

Abstract

Phasic alerting cues temporarily increase the brainâ€™s arousal state. In younger and older participants, such phasic alerting cues increase the speed of processing visual information on the perceptual level. We investigated whether intrinsic functional connectivity (iFC) in the cingulo-opercular and/ or right fronto-parietal network underlie phasic alerting effects in healthy older participants. We acquired resting-state functional magnetic resonance imaging data and performed an independent component analysis and dual regression in 31 healthy older adults. A voxel-wise multiple regression yielded that higher phasic alerting effects on visual processing speed were significantly related to lower right fronto-parietal network iFC. This result suggests a particular role of the right fronto-parietal network for phasic alertness in healthy aging.
