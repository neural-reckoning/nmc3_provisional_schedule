Abstract

Efficacy of pyruvate and tempol treatment in traumatic brain injury rat model

Patients with traumatic brain injury are at risk of developing hyperexcitability. Our study is aimed at exploring new ways to mitigate the effects of the brain damage. The results show that pyruvate and tempol application can alleviate the symptoms in rat models.
To evaluate the influence of the treatment we measured local field potential in the CA1 region of the hippocampus in rats exposed to brain injury and performed a set of behavioural tests.
Pyruvate and tempol administered orally during the first days after the injury are shown to significantly reduce neurological malfunctions and slightly lessen sensory-motor impairment. Tempol also improves exploratory behaviour and lowers the level of anxiety in rats with injury.
