A system-theoretic approach to synaptic scaling

Saeed Aljaberi

Saeed Aljaberi, Adriano Bellotti, Fulvio Forni, Timothy O’Leary (University of Cambridge)

Abstract

A closed-loop biophysical model of synaptic scaling is proposed and analyzed using tools from control theory:
- We show that the system is stable and converges to a unique parameter-dependent equilibrium, which means that the neuron’s averaged activity is regulated to the desired level (homeostasis).
- We also show the system is robust to parametric uncertainties, such as trafficking rates, degradation, and neurite size or length. This is done using classical feedback control approaches based on transfer functions and Nyquist stability criterion.
- We discuss the modeling of synaptic scaling, developing a comparison between coarse and fine models. We use the $H_{\infty}$ norm to capture the worst case error between the two models. Our findings show that the coarse model provides a good fit and captures the qualitative behavior of the complex fine model.
Based on these findings, we map complex topologies of real morphologies to coarse/reduced models and we show how to use these reduced models to predict the behavior of real neuronal morphologies without the need of computationally expensive simulations.
